About Me

I'm a postgraduate student at the University of Durham, studying for an MSc in Particles, Strings and Cosmology within the Centre for Particle Theory. Prior to this, I completed my undergraduate MSc in Natural Sciences (Mathematics and Computer Science) also at the University of Durham. My undergraduate thesis was titled 'Special Holonomy Manifolds in String Theory and M-Theory Reductions', supervised by Professors A.P. Braun and A. Donos. My full CV can be found here.

Research Interests

My main interests lie in geometrical perspectives of string theory and M-theory, but I also enjoy geometry independently:

  • Special Holonomy Manifolds, particularly Calabi-Yau and G2 manifolds
  • Geometric flows, particularly the Calabi and Kähler-Ricci flows
  • String Compactifications and Dualities
